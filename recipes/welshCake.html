Welsh Cake

Description

A Welsh teatime treat passed on through generations and still as popular as ever!

Ingredients

  • 225g plain flour
  • 85g caster sugar
  • 1/2 tsp mixed spice
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 50g butter (in small pieces)
  • 50g lard (in small pieces)
  • 50g currant
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • splash of milk

Steps:

  1. Put flour, sugar, mixed spice, baking powder, and a pinch of salt into a bowl. Then, with fingers, rub in the butter and lard until crumbly. Mix in the currants. Work the egg into the mixture until you have a soft dough, adding a splash of milk if it seems dry.
  2. Roll out the dough onto a lightly floured work surface to the thickness of your little finger. Then, cut out round pieces using a 6cm cutter, re-roll the trimmings.
  3. Great a flat griddle pan or heavy frying pan with the lard and place over medium heat. Cook welsh cakes in batches for 3 minutes each side. They should be golden brown, crisp, and cooked through.
  4. Now serve! Enjoy warm with butter and jam or with a sprinkle of sugar. Cakes stay fresh in a tin for up to a week.
